E-A-T: Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness for SEO

Understand E-A-T and how Google evaluates expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness. Learn how to build E-A-T signals to improve your SEO rankings.

Published: February 2024 Reading time: 10 minutes

What is E-A-T and Why It Matters for SEO

E-A-T stands for Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. These are core principles Google uses to evaluate the quality of websites and content. While E-A-T isn't directly a ranking factor, it influences how Google evaluates content quality, which directly impacts rankings.

Google's Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines explicitly mention E-A-T as a key factor in evaluating page quality. Higher E-A-T pages are rated more highly by Google's human evaluators, and these ratings inform how the algorithm treats pages.

This is especially important for YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) topics—content that could impact health, financial, legal, or safety decisions. For YMYL content, Google requires high E-A-T. A financial advice article on a site without financial credentials will rank poorly compared to the same article on a financial institution's site.

Demonstrating Expertise

Expertise means having deep knowledge of your topic. For content creators, demonstrating expertise involves showing you understand the subject matter at a high level and can explain it clearly to others.

Create Comprehensive Content: Write detailed, thorough content that goes deeper than surface-level information. Demonstrate understanding of nuances, edge cases, and complexities in your field.

Show Your Knowledge: Include details, examples, and insights that only someone with real expertise would know. Reference industry standards, methodologies, and frameworks from your field.

Update Content Regularly: Demonstrate that your knowledge is current by regularly updating content with recent information, new research, and updated best practices. Outdated content suggests lack of active expertise.

Author Credentials: Display author credentials, education, certifications, and experience. Readers should understand why the author is qualified to write about this topic. If you have relevant degrees, certifications, or years of professional experience, display this prominently.

Building Authoritativeness

Authoritativeness means being recognized as a leader in your field. It's external validation that you have expertise. Building authoritativeness involves becoming known and cited by others in your industry.

Earn Citations and Links: When other authoritative sites cite your work and link to your content, it signals that you're recognized as an authority. Focus on creating linkable content that other experts want to reference.

Get Featured in Media: Being quoted in reputable media outlets, industry publications, and news sites builds authority. If journalists and publications cite you as an expert, that's a strong authority signal.

Speak at Industry Events: Speaking at conferences, webinars, and industry events establishes you as an authority. Include information about speaking engagements on your website to demonstrate this authority.

Contribute to Authoritative Platforms: Write articles for well-known publications and platforms in your industry. Guest posts on established, respected publications transfer some of their authority to you.

Build a Social Following: A large, engaged social media following signals authority and influence. Audiences follow people they perceive as knowledgeable and worth listening to.

Establishing Trustworthiness

Trustworthiness is about being honest, transparent, and reliable. Users and search engines need to trust that your information is accurate and your intentions are good.

Display Trust Signals: Show contact information, business registration details, and physical address if you're a local business. Sites that hide who they are naturally generate less trust.

Be Transparent About Relationships: If you have affiliate partnerships, sponsorships, or conflicts of interest, disclose them clearly. Transparency about how you're compensated builds trust.

Handle User Reviews Well: Respond to customer reviews and feedback. Show that you take user concerns seriously. Websites that engage with criticism and complaints are more trustworthy than those that ignore them.

Use Secure Website Technology: Use HTTPS/SSL encryption (the padlock in the browser). Remove intrusive ads and suspicious elements that make your site look untrustworthy.

Cite Your Sources: Link to sources and cite research. Showing your work and making it easy to verify your claims builds trust. Claim-checking and sourcing signals that you care about accuracy.

E-A-T for Different Content Types

E-A-T requirements vary by content type and topic sensitivity. YMYL content (health, finance, law, safety) requires highest E-A-T. General information content requires moderate E-A-T. Entertainment or opinion content requires lower E-A-T but still benefits from credibility signals.

For medical content, having authors with medical degrees or certifications is important. For legal content, authors should have law degrees or practicing attorney status. For financial content, authors should have relevant financial credentials or experience.

Even for non-YMYL content, demonstrating expertise and trustworthiness helps rankings. A well-researched cooking article from someone with culinary training will outrank the same recipe from an unknown source.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does E-A-T directly impact search rankings?

E-A-T isn't a direct ranking algorithm factor, but it influences how Google's algorithms evaluate content quality. Higher E-A-T content ranks better because search engines recognize it as higher quality. E-A-T is one of the most important quality signals.

Can a new website build E-A-T quickly?

Building E-A-T takes time. A new website lacks the citations, links, and reputation that signal authority. However, new sites can accelerate E-A-T building by creating exceptional content, getting credentials in place, and actively building visibility in their industry.

How important is E-A-T for local businesses?

For local businesses, E-A-T is important for local SEO success. Google Local search prioritizes trustworthy, authoritative businesses. Reviews, citations, and credentials matter more than for national sites.

How do I show expertise without an official credential?

Demonstrate expertise through experience, comprehensive content, and citations. Years of hands-on experience can be as credible as formal credentials. Show your work, cite your sources, and create content that demonstrates deep knowledge.

Conclusion

E-A-T is foundational to modern SEO success. By demonstrating genuine expertise through comprehensive content, building authoritativeness through citations and recognition, and establishing trustworthiness through transparency and quality, you create a strong foundation for SEO rankings. E-A-T isn't something you do once—it's an ongoing commitment to quality, accuracy, and reputation building. Focus on becoming genuinely knowledgeable and recognized in your field, and the SEO benefits will follow naturally.
