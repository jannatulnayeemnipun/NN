Internal Linking Strategy for Better SEO

A practical roadmap for linking pages in a way that supports rankings and usability.

Published: June 2024 Reading time: 7 minutes

Why Internal Linking Matters for SEO

Internal links serve two critical functions: they help search engines crawl and understand your site structure, and they distribute page authority and ranking power across your website. A strategic internal linking structure improves both SEO performance and user experience.

Unlike external backlinks, internal links are completely within your control. You can strategically link pages to improve their rankings and guide search engine crawlers to your most important pages.

Anchor Text Strategy

The anchor text (clickable text in a link) tells search engines what the linked page is about. Use descriptive anchor text that includes your target keyword when appropriate, but avoid over-optimization. Natural, varied anchor text looks more legitimate to search engines.

Best practices for anchor text:

  • Use descriptive, keyword-rich anchor text
  • Vary your anchor text (don't always use the exact same text)
  • Make anchor text relevant to the linked page
  • Avoid generic text like "click here"
  • Keep anchor text concise
  • Link from high-authority pages to target pages

Site Architecture and Structure

Your site structure should be hierarchical and logical. Important pages should be closer to the homepage (fewer clicks away). This helps distribute authority and makes crawling more efficient.

Use clear category and navigation structures to help users and search engines understand your site organization. Breadcrumb navigation is particularly helpful for e-commerce and large websites.

Linking Strategies for SEO Success

Link from high-authority pages to pages you want to rank. If your homepage has the most authority, link from there to important target pages. Use contextual links within your content rather than only linking from navigation menus.

Create pillar pages that comprehensively cover broad topics, then link to cluster content that covers specific subtopics. This establishes topical authority and improves rankings for both broad and specific keywords.

Avoiding Common Linking Mistakes

Don't over-optimize by linking with the exact same anchor text repeatedly. Don't create artificial links just for SEO purposes. Don't link to irrelevant pages. Keep links natural and user-focused.

Fix broken internal links regularly. Broken links frustrate users and waste crawl budget. Use tools like Google Search Console to find crawl errors and fix them.

Conclusion

Internal linking is one of the most controllable SEO factors on your website. By strategically planning your internal links, using descriptive anchor text, and maintaining a logical site structure, you'll improve both SEO performance and user experience.
