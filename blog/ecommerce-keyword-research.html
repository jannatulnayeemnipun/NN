E-Commerce Keyword Research: Find High-Converting Buying Intent Keywords

Learn proven strategies to research e-commerce keywords that convert to sales. Identify buyer intent keywords and build a winning product keyword strategy.

Published: January 2024 Reading time: 14 minutes

Why E-Commerce Keyword Research Is Different

Traditional keyword research prioritizes search volume. E-commerce keyword research prioritizes buying intent and conversion likelihood. A keyword with 1,000 monthly searches that converts 2% is worth far more than a 10,000-search keyword that converts 0.1%.

The best e-commerce keywords indicate purchase readiness. Users searching "buy running shoes," "running shoes price," or "best running shoes" are much closer to buying than users searching "how to choose running shoes" (informational keyword). Your research should identify and prioritize these buying-intent keywords.

E-commerce keyword research also differs in scope. You're not just researching a few primary keywords—you need hundreds or thousands of product and category keywords to maximize organic revenue. This requires systematic research processes and tools that scale.

Identify Product Keyword Categories

E-commerce keywords fall into several categories that should be researched separately: product keywords (specific products), category keywords (product types), comparison keywords (comparing options), and brand keywords (specific brands).

Product keywords target specific items you sell: "blue Nike running shoes size 10" or "stainless steel water bottle 32oz." These keywords have lower search volume but high conversion intent.

Category keywords target broader product types: "running shoes," "water bottles," "athletic shirts." These keywords have higher volume but lower conversion. Use category pages to target these keywords.

Comparison keywords indicate users comparing options: "Nike vs Adidas running shoes," "hydro flask vs Nalgene water bottles." These keywords show buying intent and are worth targeting with comparison content and product pages.

Use Keyword Research Tools Effectively

Keyword research tools provide search volume, competition, and keyword difficulty metrics. Popular tools include Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs, SEMrush, and Moz. Start with Google Keyword Planner (free with Google Ads account) to understand search volumes.

Filter for high-intent keywords. Most tools show intent modifiers, allowing you to filter for "commercial" intent keywords. These keywords indicate user readiness to buy and should be prioritized in your research.

Look for long-tail keywords. Keywords with 100-1,000 monthly searches often have better conversion rates than high-volume keywords with generic intent. Long-tail keywords are easier to rank for and attract qualified users.

Analyze keyword difficulty. Target keywords where you have a realistic chance of ranking. For new e-commerce sites, prioritize medium-difficulty keywords (not the most competitive but not zero-difficulty either).

Research Competitor Keywords

Analyze competitors' keyword rankings to identify opportunities. Which keywords rank your competitors in top 10? Which keywords have high search volume but no strong competition? These represent opportunities for your site.

Use SEMrush or Ahrefs to see competitor ranking keywords. Filter for keywords you don't rank for yet—these are opportunities. Prioritize keywords with decent search volume and ranking difficulty that's achievable for your site authority.

Analyze competitor product pages. What keywords do they target with individual product pages? What keywords drive traffic to their category pages? This reveals keyword distribution strategy worth emulating.

Identify keyword gaps. Are there buying-intent keywords competitors ignore? These represent white space opportunities where you can establish rankings before competition intensifies.

Leverage Search Behavior and Autocomplete

Google's search autocomplete and related searches reveal what users actually search for. When you type in Google search, the autocomplete suggestions show real search queries with volume. Use these to discover long-tail product keywords.

Check Google's "People also ask" section on search results. The questions shown here reveal user intent and question-based keywords you should target. These keywords often convert well because they address specific concerns.

Use Amazon search autocomplete for e-commerce keyword research. Amazon's autocomplete reflects actual product purchase searches. Type product names and note what appears—these are high-intent keywords worth targeting.

Review social media and forums where customers discuss products. Reddit, Facebook groups, and product forums reveal language customers use to discuss and search for products. This natural language guides keyword selection.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's more important: search volume or buying intent?

Buying intent is more important. A 500-volume buying-intent keyword might drive more sales than a 5,000-volume informational keyword. Always prioritize keywords that indicate purchase readiness over pure search volume.

How do I find long-tail e-commerce keywords?

Use Google Keyword Planner with your product names. Sort by search volume ascending to find long-tail variations. Check autocomplete suggestions and related searches. Analyze competitor keywords for long-tail variations they rank for.

Should I target brand keywords?

Yes, if you sell that brand. Brand keywords have high buying intent. Target brand keywords for products you carry, but also target non-brand keywords where you can compete. A balanced mix of brand and non-brand keywords is ideal.

How do I prioritize keywords for my site?

Score keywords on: search volume, conversion likelihood, ranking difficulty, and strategic fit. High volume + high conversion + achievable difficulty = priority keyword. Medium volume + very high conversion + low difficulty = also priority.

How many keywords should I target?

There's no limit. Larger e-commerce sites can profitably target thousands of product and category keywords. Start with your top 100 priority keywords and expand gradually. Build keyword clusters that support internal linking strategy.

Conclusion

E-commerce keyword research requires different thinking than traditional SEO. By prioritizing buying intent over raw search volume, researching competitor opportunities, and systematically building out keyword clusters, you create a foundation for sustainable organic revenue growth.
