Featured Snippets & Position Zero: Rank Above #1

Master featured snippet optimization to earn position zero rankings. Learn snippet types and strategies to capture Google's featured snippets.

Published: March 2024 Reading time: 9 minutes

What are Featured Snippets?

Featured snippets are special search results that appear above the #1 organic result. Google extracts an answer directly from a website and displays it prominently, along with a link to the source. Featured snippets appear for queries where Google identifies a clear answer that satisfies search intent.

Featured snippets are sometimes called "position zero" because they appear before the first organic result. The website appearing in a featured snippet still gets clicks, but the snippet gives users the quick answer without clicking. This provides both visibility benefits (appearing above #1) and challenges (some users get answers without clicking).

Featured snippets significantly increase brand awareness and search visibility. Even if click-through rates aren't spectacular, appearing above #1 dramatically increases visibility and brand awareness for your keywords.

Types of Featured Snippets

Paragraph Snippets: Text answers from your content. "What is SEO?" pulls a paragraph that defines SEO. Usually 40-60 words. Most common snippet type.

List Snippets: Numbered or bulleted lists. "How to make coffee" might pull a numbered list of steps. Lists are great for how-to content and process explanations.

Table Snippets: Data presented in table format. "Best smartphones 2024" might show a table comparing phones, specs, and prices. Tables work well for comparisons and specifications.

Video Snippets: Video results for visual queries. How-to videos and demonstrations commonly earn video snippets. Proper video optimization and transcripts help with video snippets.

Optimizing for Featured Snippets

Target Question Keywords: Featured snippets appear for questions. Focus on keywords phrased as questions: "What is," "How to," "Why," "When," etc. Answer these questions clearly and concisely in your content.

Provide Clear Answers: In the first 40-60 words, provide a direct answer to the question. Don't bury the answer deep in the article. Google pulls the most relevant answer from your content—make sure the answer is obvious and prominent.

Use Appropriate Formatting: Structure content to match snippet type. For list-based answers, use actual lists (not paragraphs describing lists). For tables, use actual tables, not paragraphs describing data. Format enables Google to extract and display snippets.

Focus on Ranking Pages: Featured snippets usually come from pages already ranking for the keyword (often positions 2-10, occasionally position 1). Improve rankings for target keywords first, then optimize for snippets.

Featured Snippet Best Practices

Create content longer than the minimum snippet length. Snippets are pulled from longer content. While the snippet itself is short, the full article should be comprehensive. Longer content allows Google options for which section to pull.

Cover multiple angles and related questions. If optimizing for "What is content marketing," also address "Why is content marketing important," "Content marketing strategies," etc. Comprehensive coverage increases snippet opportunities.

Use natural language. Write for humans first, not search engines. Snippet-optimized content should read naturally while providing clear answers. Awkwardly formatted content for snippets looks bad and converts poorly.

Monitor your snippet performance. Use Google Search Console to track featured snippet impressions and clicks. Track which keywords show snippets and which don't. Use this data to optimize further.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do featured snippets increase traffic?

It varies. Some featured snippets increase traffic significantly. Others reduce clicks because users get the answer from the snippet without clicking. Ideally, featured snippets provide visibility that leads to other conversions.

Can I opt out of featured snippets?

Yes, using the "data-nosnippet" HTML tag prevents your content from being included in snippets. However, this reduces visibility. Usually, earning snippets is better than opting out.

How many snippets can one site have?

One site can rank for multiple featured snippets across different keywords. Some sites earn 10+ snippets. The goal is earning snippets for all relevant question-based keywords in your niche.

How long does it take to earn a featured snippet?

It varies. Sometimes optimizing a ranking page for snippets shows results within weeks. Other times it takes months. The page must rank well enough that Google considers it for snippet extraction.

Conclusion

Featured snippets provide significant visibility benefits by appearing above organic results. By targeting question-based keywords, providing clear answers, using appropriate formatting, and optimizing ranking pages, you can earn featured snippets that increase brand visibility and traffic from your most valuable keywords.
