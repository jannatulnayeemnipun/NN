E-Commerce Backlink Strategy: Build Authority and Dominate Rankings

Learn proven e-commerce backlink strategies. Build quality links that establish authority, boost product rankings, and drive sustainable organic revenue.

Published: January 2024 Reading time: 13 minutes

Why E-Commerce Backlinks Matter

Backlinks are among the strongest ranking signals. Google treats links as votes of confidence—more authoritative links signal that your site is trustworthy and worthy of ranking. In competitive e-commerce niches, quality backlinks often determine who ranks #1.

E-commerce sites face particular link-building challenges. Product pages are often not link-worthy to journalists or bloggers. Buying guides are more link-worthy than product pages. Link strategy requires finding creative ways to earn links through content that people want to reference.

Consistent link building compounds over time. Sites with 100+ backlinks from authoritative sources typically outrank sites with only internal linking, regardless of on-page optimization. Building links is a long-term investment that pays dividends.

Create Link-Worthy Content

The foundation of link building is creating content worth linking to. Product pages rarely get links. Buying guides, comparisons, reviews, and research get links. Invest in content that helps people and journalists reference your expertise.

Create original research. Conduct surveys, analysis, or studies relevant to your industry. "State of Online Shopping Report" or "Sustainable Fashion Analysis" generates links from news sites, blogs, and industry publications. Original data and insights are inherently link-worthy.

Create comprehensive guides. Detailed, authoritative guides that thoroughly cover topics become reference material others link to. Your buying guides and how-to content should be the best resources available on the topic.

Create infographics. Visual content is highly shareable and linkable. Infographics about industry trends, product comparisons, or statistical breakdowns are frequently linked and shared.

Develop Strategic Partnerships and Relationships

Backlinks come from relationships. Build relationships with influencers, bloggers, journalists, and industry leaders in your space. When you have genuine relationships, links follow naturally.

Partner with complementary brands. If you sell running shoes, partner with running blogs, fitness coaches, and athletic apparel brands. Cross-promotion and guest content create natural linking opportunities.

Sponsor relevant events or causes. Sponsorships often include website recognition and links. Sponsor local events, charity initiatives, or industry conferences relevant to your business. This builds community while earning links.

Collaborate on content. Create guest articles, co-authored research, or interviews with industry experts. Collaborative content benefits from both partners' networks and generates links from both audiences.

Leverage PR and Media Coverage

News coverage and press mentions provide high-authority backlinks. If you can get featured in industry publications or news outlets, those links are extremely valuable. Build newsworthy stories about your business.

Develop media relationships. Connect with journalists and bloggers covering your industry. Provide them story ideas, expert commentary, and access to your research. Regular positive relationships result in more coverage.

Create press-worthy events or announcements. New product launches, industry reports, charitable initiatives, and milestone achievements are news-worthy. Promote newsworthy developments to media and bloggers.

Monitor for unlinked mentions. Use tools to track mentions of your brand, products, or team members online. Contact sites mentioning you without linking and request a link. Many will happily add one.

Build Authority Strategically

Focus on building domain authority first. Links to your homepage and high-authority pages flow authority throughout your site. Build links strategically on your most important pages first.

Distribute authority strategically. Use internal linking to pass authority from high-authority pages to target product pages. Homepage → Category → Products creates a distribution strategy that boosts product rankings.

Avoid low-quality links. Links from unrelated, spammy, or low-quality sites can hurt more than help. Quality matters far more than quantity. Focus on links from relevant, authoritative sites.

Monitor link profile. Use SEMrush or Ahrefs to audit your backlinks. Remove low-quality links using Google Search Console's disavow tool. A clean, high-quality link profile ranks better than a large profile filled with spammy links.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many backlinks do I need to rank?

There's no magic number. Depends on competition and domain authority. Competitive niches may require 50-100 quality links. Less competitive niches might rank with 10-20 links. Focus on quality over quantity.

Should I buy backlinks?

No. Purchased links violate Google guidelines and can result in ranking penalties. Build links organically through content, relationships, and PR. Legitimate link building takes time but produces sustainable results.

How long does it take to see results from link building?

Link effects appear within weeks but compound over months. Initial links may provide 10-20% ranking boost. As link profile builds, benefits accelerate. Most significant ranking improvements appear after 3-6 months of consistent link building.

Should I request links from competitors' competitors?

Yes, strategically. Sites linking to competitors are already interested in your industry. Competitor link analysis reveals who's linking to competitors. Reach out to these sites with better content or partnership opportunities.

What's the value of nofollow vs. dofollow links?

Dofollow links pass ranking authority. Nofollow links don't pass authority but provide referral traffic and brand exposure. Both have value—mix of dofollow and nofollow links looks natural. Focus on quality regardless of follow status.

Conclusion

E-commerce backlink strategy requires creating link-worthy content, building relationships, earning media coverage, and distributing authority strategically. Quality backlinks provide sustainable competitive advantage and are often the difference between ranking #1 and ranking #5. Invest in link building as part of long-term e-commerce growth strategy.
