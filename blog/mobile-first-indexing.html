Mobile First Indexing Strategy

Prepare your website for mobile-first crawling with practical SEO fixes.

Published: September 2024 Reading time: 7 minutes

Understanding Mobile First Indexing

Google now crawls and indexes websites using the mobile version primarily. Your mobile site quality and performance directly impact rankings.

This means mobile optimization isn't optional—it's essential for SEO success.

Mobile Usability Checklist

Ensure your mobile site: has readable text without zooming, has clickable elements spaced properly, isn't blocked by intrusive interstitials, loads quickly, and has proper viewport settings.

Content Parity

Your mobile site should have the same content as your desktop site. Don't hide important content from mobile users. Google uses the mobile version to understand your pages.

Mobile Performance

Mobile users have slower connections. Optimize images aggressively and minimize render-blocking resources.

Mobile Testing

Use Google's Mobile Friendly Test and PageSpeed Insights to identify mobile issues. Test on real devices with slower networks.

Conclusion

Mobile-first indexing has made mobile optimization critical. Prioritize mobile user experience and performance for better rankings.
